Universal Equipment & Safety

Required Equipment (for all injectable peptides)

Insulin Syringes: 29g or 31g × 1/2" (1cc/100 unit)
Bacteriostatic Water (BAC): For reconstitution only — never use sterile water
Alcohol Swabs: 70% isopropyl, for vial tops and injection sites
Sharps Container: For safe needle disposal
Refrigerator: Store reconstituted peptides at 36–46°F (2–8°C)
Digital Thermometer: Verify fridge temperature

Universal Reconstitution Method

  • Step 1: Remove peptide vial and BAC water from fridge. Let peptide vial reach room temperature (~5 min). Do not shake.
  • Step 2: Swab the top of both the peptide vial and the BAC water vial with an alcohol pad.
  • Step 3: Draw the desired amount of BAC water into an insulin syringe (see individual peptide sections for exact amounts).
  • Step 4: Insert needle into the peptide vial at an angle, aiming at the glass wall — NOT directly onto the powder. Let the water trickle down the side of the vial slowly.
  • Step 5: Do NOT shake. Gently swirl or roll the vial between your palms until the powder is fully dissolved. This may take 1–3 minutes. The solution should be clear.
  • Step 6: Label the vial with: peptide name, reconstitution date, concentration (mcg per unit), and discard date (28 days from reconstitution).

Injection Technique (Subcutaneous)

  • Sites: Lower abdomen (2 inches from navel), outer thigh, or rear deltoid area. Rotate sites daily to prevent lipodystrophy.
  • Technique: Pinch a fold of skin, insert needle at 45° angle, inject slowly (5–10 seconds), hold for 3 seconds, withdraw.
  • Timing: Most peptides are best administered fasted (2+ hours since last meal) unless otherwise noted.
  • Never reuse needles. One syringe per injection. Dispose in sharps container immediately.

Storage Rules

  • Unreconstituted (lyophilized powder): Room temperature for short term, refrigerated for long term, freezer for 6+ months
  • Reconstituted: Refrigerate immediately. Use within 28 days. Never freeze reconstituted peptides.
  • Never expose to direct sunlight, extreme heat, or repeated temperature cycling.
  • If the solution becomes cloudy, discolored, or has particles after reconstitution — discard. Do not use.

CJC-1295 with DAC

CJC-1295 with DAC
Also known as: Modified GRF(1-29) with Drug Affinity Complex · DAC:GRF
GH Releasing
A long-acting growth hormone releasing hormone (GHRH) analog that stimulates your pituitary gland to produce more of your own growth hormone naturally. The DAC (Drug Affinity Complex) binds to albumin in the blood, extending the half-life from ~30 minutes to approximately 8 days — allowing for convenient once-weekly dosing with sustained, steady GH elevation.

What It Does

CJC-1295 with DAC signals your pituitary gland to release growth hormone in a sustained fashion. Unlike synthetic HGH which provides a flat, supraphysiological dose, CJC-1295 amplifies your body's own GH production. Growth hormone affects virtually every system: it drives fat metabolism (particularly visceral and subcutaneous fat), supports lean muscle growth and repair, enhances deep wave sleep, improves skin quality and collagen production, strengthens bone density, and supports cognitive function. GH levels naturally decline ~15% per decade after age 30 — CJC-1295 reverses this decline.

Reconstitution (10mg vial)

  • Add 2mL (200 units) of bacteriostatic water to the 10mg vial
  • This gives you a concentration of 5mg per mL (5,000mcg per mL, or 50mcg per unit on an insulin syringe)
  • For a 1mg dose: draw 20 units. For a 2mg dose: draw 40 units.
  • One 10mg vial = 5–10 weekly doses depending on your protocol

Dosing Strategies

ProtocolDoseFrequencyBest For
Conservative1mgOnce weeklyAnti-aging, sleep, skin, general wellness
Standard (Recommended)1.5mgOnce weeklyRecomp, fat loss, muscle recovery
Aggressive2mgOnce weeklyMaximum GH elevation, advanced users
  • Timing: Inject before bed on an empty stomach (fasted 2+ hours). This amplifies the natural nighttime GH pulse.
  • Consistency matters more than dose. Pick a day of the week and stick to it.

Cycling

  • Recommended cycle: 12–16 weeks on, 4–8 weeks off. This prevents pituitary desensitization.
  • Extended use: Some protocols run 6 months continuous with periodic blood work to monitor IGF-1 levels.
  • Off-cycle: GH levels will gradually return to baseline over 2–3 weeks after cessation.

Stacking & Pairing

  • Excellent with: Tesamorelin (synergistic GH release), BPC-157 (recovery), MOTS-c (metabolic), NAD+ (energy/longevity)
  • Classic combo: CJC-1295 DAC + Tesamorelin = the two most powerful GH-releasing peptides combined. CJC provides the sustained baseline elevation; Tesamorelin adds pulsatile visceral-fat-targeting GH release.
  • Works alongside: GLP-1 drugs (Reta, semaglutide), testosterone therapy, thyroid medications

Contraindications & Cautions

  • Do not use if: Active cancer or history of cancer (GH can accelerate tumor growth), untreated pituitary disorders
  • Use caution with: Diabetes (GH can increase insulin resistance — monitor blood glucose), carpal tunnel syndrome (may worsen), edema-prone conditions
  • Do not combine with: Synthetic HGH at the same time (redundant and can suppress natural production). Choose one approach.
  • Blood work: Check IGF-1 and fasting glucose at baseline, 8 weeks, and 16 weeks

Realistic Expectations — What You Will (and Won't) Feel

  • Week 1–2: Most people notice improved sleep quality first — deeper, more restful sleep with more vivid dreams. You may wake up feeling more refreshed. This is the earliest and most reliable sign it's working.
  • Week 3–4: Subtle improvements in skin quality (less dryness, more elasticity), faster recovery from workouts, slightly more energy throughout the day.
  • Week 6–8: Noticeable changes in body composition if combined with training and nutrition — slightly leaner, muscles feel fuller. Nails and hair may grow faster.
  • Week 12+: Cumulative benefits become significant — measurable fat loss, improved lean mass, better bloodwork markers.
  • What you WON'T feel: There is no immediate "rush" or acute sensation from CJC-1295. It is not a stimulant. If someone tells you they "feel it working" within minutes, that's placebo. The benefits are gradual, cumulative, and best measured objectively (body scans, bloodwork, photos) rather than by subjective "feeling."
  • Possible side effects: Mild water retention in the first 1–2 weeks (resolves), occasional injection site redness, rare: tingling in hands/feet (reduce dose if persistent).

BPC-157 Body Protection Compound

BPC-157
Body Protection Compound-157 · Gastric Pentadecapeptide · Bepecin
Healing & Repair
A 15-amino-acid peptide derived from a naturally occurring protective protein found in human gastric juice. BPC-157 is your body's own repair signal, amplified. It dramatically accelerates the healing of tendons, ligaments, muscles, gut lining, and even bone. According to the FBF Cycle Design Guide: "BPC-157: 250-500mcg daily — protects gut from heavy food intake, heals tendons under heavy loads."

What It Does

BPC-157 promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation) at injury sites, upregulates growth factor receptors (VEGF, FGF, EGF), and modulates the nitric oxide system. It has powerful anti-inflammatory effects without the side effects of NSAIDs. It repairs the GI tract, which is especially valuable for clients on GLP-1 medications that can cause gut irritation. It also has neuroprotective properties — protecting dopamine neurons and reducing anxiety-like behavior in research. It is one of the most well-tolerated peptides with virtually no reported adverse effects across extensive research.

  • Concentration: 10mg per mL (10,000mcg per mL, or 100mcg per unit)
  • For 250mcg dose: draw 2.5 units. For 500mcg: draw 5 units.
  • One 20mg vial at 375mcg/day = ~53 days of use

  • Recovery stack: BPC-157 + CJC-1295 DAC + GHK-Cu = accelerated healing from multiple pathways
  • Works alongside: Virtually everything. BPC-157 has no known negative interactions.

  • Caution with active cancer: BPC-157 promotes angiogenesis (blood vessel growth). Theoretically, this could support tumor vascularization. If you have active cancer, discuss with your oncologist.
  • Pregnancy/nursing: Not studied — avoid.
  • No known drug interactions. One of the safest peptides available.

Realistic Expectations

  • Day 1–3: You will not feel anything acutely. No sensation, no rush, no warmth. If you're expecting to "feel" BPC-157 working, you won't.
  • Week 1–2: If you have gut issues (bloating, acid reflux, IBS symptoms), you may notice improvement within the first 7–14 days. Gut healing is the fastest observable benefit.
  • Week 2–4: Tendon and joint pain begins to decrease. Injuries that have been nagging for months start to feel better. Range of motion improves.
  • Week 4–8: Significant healing progress on tendons, ligaments, and muscle injuries. Some clients report injuries they've had for years finally resolving.
  • What you WON'T feel: Energy boost, mood change, appetite change, or any stimulant-like effect. BPC-157 is purely a repair peptide. Its benefits are measured by reduction in pain, faster healing times, and improved gut function — not by subjective "feeling."
  • Side effects: Extremely rare. Occasional mild nausea if injected on a completely empty stomach. Rare injection site redness.

Retatrutide (Reta)

Retatrutide
Reta · LY3437943 · Triple Agonist GLP-1
Weight Management · Triple Agonist
The most advanced weight management compound available — a triple receptor agonist that simultaneously activates GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon receptors. GIP receptor activation enhances insulin secretion and fat metabolism. Glucagon receptor activation — the unique third mechanism — directly drives visceral fat oxidation, increases resting metabolic rate, and promotes liver fat burning. Clinical trials showed up to 24% body weight loss at 48 weeks. It also significantly improves insulin sensitivity, HbA1c, cholesterol panel (LDL, triglycerides, total cholesterol), liver enzymes, and blood pressure.

  • Common side effects: Nausea (especially weeks 1–4), constipation or diarrhea, acid reflux. All typically resolve with titration and time. BPC-157 can help manage GI effects.
