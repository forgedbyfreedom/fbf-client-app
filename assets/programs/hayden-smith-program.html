FBF Recomp Protocol

HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Body Recomposition — The Optimal Path

Rather than a pure cut, we strongly recommend the FBF Recomp Protocol. At 235 lbs with a high training volume (lifting + BJJ), you are in an ideal position to simultaneously lose body fat and build lean muscle. This approach preserves performance on the mat, avoids muscle loss from aggressive dieting, and produces a more dramatic visual transformation while keeping you strong and competitive.

How the Recomp Protocol Works

  • High Protein Anchor (240g/day): Protein stays constant every single day. At 235 lbs with high training volume, you need ~1g/lb to drive muscle protein synthesis and recovery from both lifting and BJJ.
  • Strategic Carb Placement: Carbs are timed around training and rolling sessions (pre/post) to fuel performance and glycogen replenishment. Higher carbs on BJJ days, slightly lower on rest days.
  • Moderate Caloric Deficit: 2,400 calories keeps you in a deficit for fat loss (~400–500 below maintenance given your activity level) without compromising mat performance or recovery.
  • Progressive Overload Training: The 4-day lifting program builds the strength and muscle that makes you dangerous on the mat. BJJ alone won't build the structural strength you need.
  • BJJ Synergy: Your rolling sessions provide high-intensity cardio, grip endurance, and sport-specific conditioning. Combined with structured lifting, this is the ultimate recomp environment.
  • Peptide Support: The recommended peptide stack accelerates fat oxidation, supports joint/tissue recovery (critical for grapplers), and enhances sleep quality — all drivers of recomp.

Expected Timeline

Weeks 1–4: Body adapts, strength begins climbing, scale may not move much (this is normal — recomp is happening). Weeks 5–12: Visible changes in the mirror, clothes fit differently, strength PRs weekly, rolling feels easier at a lighter weight. Weeks 12–24: 25–30 lbs of fat lost, significant lean mass gained. Target weight of 205–210 lbs achieved with dramatically improved body composition, mat performance, and gas tank.

BJJ-Specific Training Notes

  • Grip Strength: Towel grip rows, farmer's carries, and dead hangs are built into the program. Your grip is your first line of offense and defense on the mat.
  • Neck Strengthening: Neck curls protect against guillotines, cranks, and general grappling forces. Start light and build gradually.
  • Rotational Core: Pallof presses and cable woodchops develop the anti-rotation and rotational power needed for sweeps, guard retention, and scrambles.
  • Hip Mobility: 90/90 switches at the end of lower body days keep your hips open for guard work. Add thoracic spine rotations during warm-ups.
  • Shoulder Prehab: Face pulls and band pull-aparts every upper body day. Your shoulders take enormous stress from gripping, framing, and being stacked. Prehab prevents missed mat time.

Cardio Protocol

BJJ-Integrated

BJJ as Primary Cardio

  • BJJ Training (3–4x/week): Your rolling sessions ARE your high-intensity cardio. A 90-minute BJJ class with live rounds is equivalent to or exceeds any HIIT protocol. Do not add additional HIIT on BJJ days.
  • Non-BJJ Days — Incline Walk: 20–25 min incline treadmill walk (10–12% incline, 3.0–3.5 mph). Low-impact, promotes recovery blood flow, burns fat without taxing your CNS or joints.
  • Daily Step Target: 10,000 steps minimum. Walk the dog, take calls walking, park far. Steps are your fat-loss foundation.
  • Weekends (Active Recovery): Light rolling, open mat, mobility/yoga, higher step goals (12,000+ steps). No intense lifting on weekends.

Hydration & Electrolytes — CRITICAL for BJJ Athletes

You sweat more than almost any other athlete category. A 90-minute rolling session can dump 2–4 lbs of water weight. Dehydration kills performance, cognition, and recovery. 120+ oz water daily is your minimum. On BJJ days, add an extra LMNT packet before or during class. Weigh yourself before and after rolling — every pound lost is ~16oz of water you need to replace.

Peptide Protocol

FBF RECOMP STACK

Recommended Recomp Peptide Stack

This peptide combination is the backbone of the FBF Recomp Protocol. It enhances fat burning, dramatically improves recovery (critical for BJJ athletes), deepens sleep, and accelerates tissue repair from the daily micro-trauma of grappling. All peptides are administered via subcutaneous (SubQ) injection only.

CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin

300mcg CJC / 300mcg Ipamorelin
Subcutaneous injection — before bed (fasted 2+ hours)
Growth hormone releasing peptide combo. Enhances deep sleep, accelerates fat loss, supports lean muscle growth. The cornerstone of the recomp stack. Does not interfere with natural GH pulsatility. BJJ Relevance: Improved recovery between sessions means you can train harder and more frequently. Better sleep = faster central nervous system recovery after intense rolling.

BPC-157

250–500mcg/day
Subcutaneous injection — AM or split AM/PM
HUGE for BJJ athletes. Accelerates tissue repair, tendon/ligament healing, gut health, and joint recovery. Grapplers put enormous stress on fingers, wrists, shoulders, elbows, and knees. BPC-157 speeds repair of all connective tissue. This is your #1 longevity peptide for combat sports. Can be injected near the site of injury for localized benefit.

Tesamorelin

2mg/day
Subcutaneous injection — before bed (fasted)
FDA-approved GHRH analog. Specifically targets visceral (belly) fat reduction. Ideal for recomp — preserves lean mass while reducing the most dangerous fat compartment. Synergistic with CJC/Ipamorelin for enhanced GH release. BJJ Relevance: Better power-to-weight ratio on the mat without sacrificing strength.

Thymosin Alpha-1

1.6mg 2x/week
Subcutaneous injection — any time
Immune modulator. BJJ gyms are petri dishes — staph, ringworm, and respiratory infections are constant risks. Thymosin Alpha-1 enhances immune resilience and T-cell function. BJJ Relevance: Constant skin-to-skin contact and shared mats mean your immune system needs every advantage.

PT-141 (Bremelanotide)

1–2mg as needed
Subcutaneous injection — 1-2 hours before desired effect
Melanocortin receptor agonist for sexual wellness and libido enhancement. Works centrally via the nervous system rather than vascularly. Use as needed, not daily. Do not exceed 2mg per dose or use more than 8 doses per month.

Thymosin Beta-4 (TB-500)

2.5–5mg 2x/week
Subcutaneous injection — AM or PM
Powerful tissue repair, anti-inflammatory, and flexibility-enhancing peptide. Promotes angiogenesis and cell migration to injury sites. BJJ Relevance: Perfect for grapplers — helps repair the constant micro-tears in muscles, tendons, and ligaments from rolling. Improves flexibility and range of motion for guard play and submissions. Synergistic with BPC-157 for accelerated healing.

GHK-Cu

1–2mg/day
Subcutaneous injection — AM
Copper peptide for skin rejuvenation, collagen synthesis, and wound healing. Stimulates production of glycosaminoglycans, decorin, and other structural proteins. BJJ Relevance: Accelerates healing of mat burns, gi rash, and skin abrasions that are constant in grappling. Supports connective tissue integrity long-term.

Epithalon

5–10mg/day SubQ (10–20 day cycles)
Subcutaneous injection — AM or before bed
Telomere lengthening peptide. Activates telomerase to protect chromosomal integrity. Anti-aging, improved sleep quality, and enhanced melatonin production. Run in 10–20 day cycles, then take 4–6 months off. BJJ Relevance: Better sleep quality = faster recovery. Longevity peptide for athletes who want to train into their 40s, 50s, and beyond.

Selank

250–500mcg/day
Subcutaneous injection ONLY — NOT nasal
Anxiolytic and cognitive-enhancing peptide. Modulates GABA, serotonin, and dopamine. Reduces anxiety without sedation or cognitive impairment. Administered via SubQ injection only. BJJ Relevance: Competition anxiety management — stay calm and focused under pressure. Enhances strategic thinking during matches without the fog of traditional anti-anxiety compounds.

Semax

200–600mcg/day
Subcutaneous injection ONLY — NOT nasal
Nootropic peptide derived from ACTH. Enhances BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor), improves memory, focus, and neuroprotection. Administered via SubQ injection only. BJJ Relevance: BJJ is chess with your body — Semax sharpens pattern recognition, reaction time, and the ability to chain techniques. Ideal for drilling and competition prep.

MOTS-c

5–10mg SubQ 3x/week
Subcutaneous injection — AM, preferably on training days
Mitochondrial-derived peptide and exercise mimetic. Activates AMPK, improves insulin sensitivity, enhances fatty acid oxidation, and optimizes metabolic function. BJJ Relevance: Improves muscular endurance and metabolic efficiency — critical for 5-minute rounds. Helps your cells produce energy more efficiently so you don't gas out during late rounds.

AOD-9604

300mcg/day
Subcutaneous injection — AM on empty stomach
Modified fragment of human growth hormone (HGH fragment 176-191). Specifically stimulates lipolysis and inhibits lipogenesis without affecting blood sugar or growth. BJJ Relevance: Clean fat loss without muscle catabolism — make weight for competition while maintaining strength and mat performance. Stacks well with Tesamorelin for enhanced body composition.

Dihexa

10–20mg SubQ 3x/week
Subcutaneous injection — AM
Research peptide — discuss with physician. Potent cognitive enhancer, reported to be 10 million times more potent than BDNF at forming new synaptic connections. Supports neuroplasticity and memory. BJJ Relevance: Accelerates learning of new techniques and positional concepts. Enhances the neural pathways that make movements automatic under pressure.

NAD+ (Injectable)

50–100mg SubQ or IV
Subcutaneous injection — AM (IV via clinic)
Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide — essential coenzyme for cellular energy production, DNA repair, and sirtuin activation. Anti-aging at the cellular level. BJJ Relevance: Faster recovery from training, improved cellular repair after hard rolling sessions. Combats the accelerated aging that high-volume training can cause. Supports mitochondrial function for sustained energy on the mat.

Pentosan Polysulfate (PPS)

2–3mg/kg SubQ weekly
Subcutaneous injection — once weekly
Semi-synthetic polysaccharide with potent chondroprotective properties. Promotes joint cartilage repair, stimulates synovial fluid production, and supports intervertebral disc regeneration. BJJ Relevance: Extremely relevant for grapplers — constant guard passing pressure, stacking, and spinal compression degrade cartilage and discs over time. PPS directly addresses the joint and spinal wear that ends BJJ careers. Protects knees, hips, and spine.

Peptide Layering Schedule

Don't start everything at once. Layer peptides in over 8 weeks to assess tolerance and response:

  • Weeks 1–2: CJC-1295/Ipamorelin + BPC-157 — establish the GH + recovery foundation
  • Weeks 3–4: Add Tesamorelin + TB-500 — layer in visceral fat targeting and deep tissue repair
  • Weeks 5–6: Add GHK-Cu + Epithalon cycle (10–20 days) — collagen, anti-aging, sleep optimization
  • Weeks 7–8: Add MOTS-c + AOD-9604 — metabolic optimization and additional fat loss
  • Layer in others as desired: Selank/Semax for competition prep, PT-141 as needed, Thymosin Alpha-1 for immune support, NAD+ for cellular energy, PPS for joint/cartilage protection, Dihexa for cognitive goals

Important Peptide Notes

  • All peptides are administered via subcutaneous (SubQ) injection ONLY — no nasal sprays for any peptide
  • Follow the layering schedule above to assess individual response and tolerance
  • Store all peptides refrigerated. Reconstitute with bacteriostatic water only
  • Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thigh, glute) to prevent lipodystrophy
  • BPC-157 and TB-500 can be injected near the site of injury/pain for localized benefit (e.g., shoulder, knee, elbow)
  • Epithalon is run in cycles (10–20 days on, 4–6 months off) — do not run continuously
  • Dihexa is a research peptide — use under physician guidance only
  • NAD+ can be administered SubQ at home or via IV infusion at a clinic for higher bioavailability
  • Coordinate with your physician — provide this protocol sheet at your next appointment

Retatrutide (Reta)

DISCUSS WITH DOC

Next-Generation Triple Agonist (GIP/GLP-1/Glucagon)

Retatrutide is a triple-hormone receptor agonist with clinical data showing profound improvements across nearly every metabolic marker. For BJJ athletes, it offers body composition improvement without sacrificing strength, cardio capacity, or mat performance.

Why Reta Matters for BJJ Athletes

  • Body composition without performance loss: Unlike aggressive cutting, Reta helps shed fat while preserving lean mass and strength — you get lighter without getting weaker
  • Visceral fat targeting: The glucagon receptor activation specifically targets the deep abdominal fat, improving your power-to-weight ratio on the mat
  • Improved insulin sensitivity: Better glucose management means more stable energy during long rolling sessions
  • Appetite regulation: Superior satiety signaling makes hitting your 2,400 cal target effortless
  • Resting metabolic rate: The glucagon receptor activation increases resting metabolic rate — you burn more calories at rest

Titration Protocol (Physician-Guided)

  • Weeks 1–4: 1mg/week subcutaneous injection — assess tolerance, monitor GI side effects
  • Weeks 5–8: 2mg/week — body adapts, appetite regulation kicks in
  • Weeks 9–12: 4mg/week — full therapeutic dose, maximum metabolic benefit
  • Weeks 13+: Maintain at 4mg or adjust per physician guidance and lab results
  • Inject once weekly, same day each week. Rotate injection sites.
  • Monitor: weight, body composition, blood glucose, GI symptoms, energy levels

Tesofensine

DISCUSS WITH DOC
  • Mechanism: Triple monoamine reuptake inhibitor — blocks reuptake of serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine
  • Fat loss: Clinical trials showed 2x the weight loss of existing medications at the time of study
  • Thermogenic: Increases resting metabolic rate and fat oxidation — you burn more calories at rest
  • Mood & Focus: Dopamine and serotonin reuptake inhibition provides significant mood elevation, mental clarity, and focus — beneficial for athletes managing training stress, work, and family
  • Appetite suppression: Powerful satiety signal that makes deficit adherence easy without feeling deprived
  • BJJ relevance: The cognitive and mood benefits help with the mental game — focus during rolls, motivation during tough training blocks, and reduced perception of effort
  • Typical dose: 0.25–0.5mg/day oral, physician-guided titration
  • Monitor: Heart rate, blood pressure, sleep quality (can be stimulating — take AM only)

Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT)

LABS FIRST
  • Why it matters for combat athletes: Recovery demands from BJJ are extreme — you're essentially doing full-body resistance training against another human 3–4x/week on top of your lifting program. Optimal testosterone levels are critical for recovery, tissue repair, and body composition.
  • Get labs first: Total testosterone, free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, LH, FSH, prolactin. Do NOT start TRT without baseline labs.
  • Signs of low T to watch for: Persistent fatigue despite good sleep, difficulty recovering between sessions, stalled strength gains, low motivation, brain fog, decreased libido
  • If labs indicate low/suboptimal T: Discuss TRT with your physician. Typical protocol: 100–200mg testosterone cypionate/week via SubQ or intramuscular injection, split into 2 doses/week for stable levels
  • Competition note: If you compete in IBJJF or other sanctioned BJJ events, be aware of anti-doping policies. Discuss with your coach and physician.
  • Monitoring: Re-test labs at 6 weeks, then every 3–6 months. Monitor hematocrit, PSA, lipids, estradiol

Recommended Lab Panel

BASELINE + ONGOING

Get these labs done before starting any compounds. Re-test at 12 weeks and every 3–6 months thereafter.

  • Metabolic Panel: Fasting glucose, fasting insulin, HbA1c
  • Lipid Panel: Total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides, VLDL
  • Liver Panel: ALT, AST, GGT, alkaline phosphatase, bilirubin
  • Kidney Function: BUN, creatinine, eGFR, uric acid
  • Thyroid: TSH, Free T3, Free T4
  • Hormones: Total testosterone, free testosterone, SHBG, estradiol, LH, FSH, prolactin

Daily Temperature Check

METABOLIC TRACKING

Why We Track Your Morning Temperature

Your waking body temperature is one of the most underused and powerful biomarkers in coaching. It gives us a direct, daily window into your metabolic rate, thyroid function, recovery status, and overall hormonal health — all without a blood draw. This single data point, tracked consistently, can reveal problems weeks before they show up on lab work or the scale.

How to Take Your Temperature

  • When: Immediately upon waking, before getting out of bed, before drinking water, before any activity
  • How: Use a digital oral thermometer. Place under your tongue for the full reading cycle (usually 30–60 seconds). Do not talk or breathe through your mouth.
  • Where to log: Record it in your daily check-in in the FBF app under the body/notes section
  • Keep your thermometer on your nightstand — if you forget and get up first, the reading is no longer valid for that day
  • Consistency is key: Same thermometer, same method, every single morning. The trend over days and weeks is what matters, not any single reading.

What Your Temperature Tells Us

Metabolic Rate & Thyroid Function

Your basal body temperature is a direct reflection of your metabolic rate — how much energy your body is producing at rest. Thyroid hormones (T3 and T4) are the primary regulators of this process.

  • Healthy range: 97.8°F – 98.6°F upon waking indicates a well-functioning metabolism and adequate thyroid output
  • Consistently below 97.6°F is a red flag for metabolic downregulation — your body is slowing its engine to conserve energy. This is the earliest warning sign that calories may be too low, the deficit has been running too long, or thyroid function is suppressed.
  • If we see a sustained drop, we can increase calories (refeed), adjust carb timing, or flag it for your physician to check thyroid labs — all before you hit a plateau or feel run down.

Adaptive Thermogenesis & Diet Response

When you diet — especially in a caloric deficit — your body can fight back through a process called adaptive thermogenesis. It literally turns down the thermostat to burn fewer calories and protect its fat stores.

  • A gradual temperature decline (0.3–0.5°F over 2–3 weeks) while in a deficit tells us your metabolism is adapting. This is normal to a degree, but if it drops too far or too fast, we need to act.
  • What we do about it: Strategic refeed days (temporary calorie increase, especially carbs) to signal to your body that it's not starving. This resets leptin signaling and bumps thermogenesis back up.
  • Why the recomp approach helps: By keeping your deficit moderate (2,400 cal, not extreme) and protein very high (240g), we minimize adaptive thermogenesis. Your body has less reason to panic because it's getting enough fuel and building blocks.

Recovery, Stress & Overtraining

Your temperature also reflects your autonomic nervous system balance. For a BJJ athlete training 3–4x/week plus 4 lifting sessions, overtraining is a real risk.

  • A sudden temperature drop (0.5°F+ from your baseline) after a hard training week can indicate overreaching — your nervous system is fatigued and recovery systems are taxed
  • A spike above your normal range can indicate acute stress, inflammation, infection onset, or an immune response
  • Pattern recognition: Over 4–6 weeks of data, we'll know your personal baseline. Then deviations become incredibly informative — we can see when your body needs a deload week, more sleep, or a protocol adjustment before you even feel it subjectively.
  • Cortisol connection: Chronically elevated cortisol (from under-recovery, poor sleep, or life stress) suppresses thyroid conversion (T4→T3) and lowers waking temperature. If we see this pattern, we address stress and recovery, not just training volume.

Hormonal Health & Peptide Response

Temperature is a downstream marker of your entire hormonal cascade — not just thyroid, but testosterone, growth hormone, insulin sensitivity, and more.

  • Tracking response to peptides: When we introduce CJC/Ipamorelin and Tesamorelin, we expect to see improved sleep, better recovery, and a stable or slightly elevated waking temperature over 4–6 weeks. If temperature doesn't respond, it tells us the peptide dose or timing may need adjustment.
  • Testosterone correlation: Low waking temperature combined with fatigue, low motivation, and stalled strength gains can indicate low testosterone — something we'd flag for bloodwork to confirm.
  • Sleep quality feedback loop: Peptides like CJC/Ipamorelin enhance deep sleep, which is when your body does the most recovery and hormone production. Improved sleep → better hormone output → higher waking temperature.

Coach Recommendations

BJJ-SPECIFIC

BJJ-Specific Recovery

  • Contrast Showers (Post-BJJ): 30 sec cold / 60 sec hot, 3–5 cycles. Reduces inflammation, speeds recovery, improves circulation. Do this after every rolling session.
  • Sleep Optimization: Target 7.5–9 hours per night. This is where ALL your recovery happens. Dark room, cool (65–68°F), no screens 30 min before bed. Magnesium glycinate + CJC/Ipamorelin before bed enhances deep sleep quality.
  • Stress Management: Between BJJ, lifting, work, and family — total stress load matters. If life stress is high, consider pulling back one BJJ session that week rather than pushing through and accumulating fatigue.
  • Skin Care: Shower immediately after every BJJ session. Use Defense Soap or tea tree body wash. Check for any cuts, scrapes, or suspicious skin changes daily. BJJ gyms carry staph and ringworm — Thymosin Alpha-1 helps, but hygiene is your first line of defense.

BJJ-Specific Mobility Work

  • Hip Mobility (daily): 90/90 switches, pigeon pose, frog stretch — 5 min minimum. Open hips = better guard, better passing, fewer back injuries.
  • Thoracic Spine (daily): Foam roller T-spine extensions, open book rotations, cat/cow — 5 min. Critical for posture, framing, and preventing upper back stiffness from grappling.
  • Shoulder Mobility (training days): Wall slides, band dislocates, sleeper stretch — 3 min before every upper body session. Your shoulders are your most vulnerable joint in BJJ.
  • Grip Recovery (post-BJJ): Finger extensor band work, rice bucket, forearm stretching. Your grip is your weapon — take care of it.
  • Neck Stretches (daily): Gentle cervical ROM work — chin tucks, lateral flexion holds, levator scapulae stretch. Never crank or force. Neck health = career longevity in grappling.
