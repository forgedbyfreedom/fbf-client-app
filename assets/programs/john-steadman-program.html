FBF Recomp Protocol

HIGHLY RECOMMENDED

Body Recomposition — The Optimal Path

Rather than a pure cut, we strongly recommend the FBF Recomp Protocol. At 204 lbs with access to GLP-1 and a structured training program, you are in an ideal position to simultaneously lose body fat and build lean muscle. This approach preserves metabolic rate, avoids the "skinny fat" outcome, and produces a more dramatic visual transformation than dieting alone.

How the Recomp Protocol Works

  • High Protein Anchor (200g/day): Protein stays constant every single day. This is non-negotiable — it drives muscle protein synthesis and keeps you full.
  • Strategic Carb Placement: Carbs are timed around training (pre/post workout) to fuel performance and recovery while keeping blood glucose manageable.
  • Moderate Caloric Deficit: 1,880 calories keeps you in a deficit for fat loss (~300–400 below maintenance) without tanking energy or recovery.
  • Progressive Overload Training: The stimulus for muscle growth comes from systematically increasing weight/reps every session. Without this, recomp doesn't work.
  • GLP-1 Synergy: Your GLP-1 medication enhances appetite regulation and insulin sensitivity — this actually makes recomp easier for you than most clients.
  • Peptide Support: The recommended peptide stack below accelerates fat oxidation, supports recovery, and enhances sleep quality — all critical drivers of recomp.

Expected Timeline

Weeks 1–4: Body adapts, strength begins climbing, scale may not move much (this is normal — recomp is happening). Weeks 5–12: Visible changes in the mirror, clothes fit differently, strength PRs weekly. Weeks 12–24: 20–24 lbs of fat lost, significant lean mass gained. Target weight of 180 lbs achieved with a dramatically different body composition than just "dieting down."

Discuss with Your Endo: Retatrutide

PHYSICIAN GUIDED

Retatrutide (Reta) — The Next-Generation GLP-1

Since you're already on a GLP-1 agonist, we strongly recommend discussing Retatrutide with your endocrinologist at your next visit. Reta is a triple-hormone receptor agonist (GIP/GLP-1/Glucagon) that represents a significant upgrade from single-target GLP-1 medications, with clinical data showing profound improvements across nearly every metabolic marker — especially relevant for Type 1 Diabetes management.

Why Retatrutide Is Worth Discussing

Retatrutide activates three hormone receptors simultaneously — GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon — creating a synergistic metabolic effect that single-agonist GLP-1 drugs cannot match. Here's what the clinical data shows:

Visceral Fat Reduction

  • Clinical trials demonstrated up to 24% body weight reduction at the 48-week mark — significantly more than existing GLP-1 medications
  • The glucagon receptor activation specifically targets visceral adipose tissue (the deep abdominal fat surrounding organs) — the most metabolically dangerous fat compartment
  • Visceral fat is directly linked to insulin resistance, cardiovascular risk, and systemic inflammation — reducing it dramatically improves all downstream health markers
  • For a T1D patient at 204 lbs targeting 180 lbs, Reta could accelerate the recomp timeline while preserving lean mass

Insulin Sensitivity & Blood Sugar Control

  • Improved insulin sensitivity: The triple-receptor mechanism enhances how your cells respond to insulin, meaning your existing insulin doses may become more effective
  • Reduced HbA1c: Clinical data shows significant HbA1c reductions, even in patients with already-managed diabetes
  • Stabilized fasting glucose: The glucagon receptor activation paradoxically improves glucose homeostasis by enhancing hepatic glucose regulation
  • Less glycemic variability: Smoother glucose curves throughout the day, fewer hypo/hyper episodes, easier to manage with insulin dosing
  • Combined with our low-carb meal plan and consistent carb timing, this could meaningfully reduce your total daily insulin requirements

Cholesterol & Cardiovascular Markers

  • Total cholesterol reduction: Significant decreases in total cholesterol observed across all dose groups in Phase 2 trials
  • LDL ("bad") cholesterol: Meaningful reductions, especially in patients with elevated baseline LDL — critical for T1D patients who carry higher cardiovascular risk
  • Triglycerides: Marked improvement (often 20–30% reduction), directly tied to reduced visceral fat and improved insulin signaling
  • HDL ("good") cholesterol: Preserved or improved, unlike some weight loss interventions that lower HDL alongside LDL
  • hs-CRP (inflammation): Reductions in systemic inflammatory markers, which are chronically elevated in T1D and contribute to long-term complications

Liver Health & Blood Markers

  • ALT & AST (liver enzymes): Significant reductions in liver transaminases, indicating decreased hepatic inflammation and improved liver function
  • Hepatic fat reduction: The glucagon receptor activation drives direct liver fat oxidation — clinical imaging studies showed dramatic reductions in liver fat content
  • NAFLD/NASH improvement: Early data suggests Reta may be one of the most effective interventions for fatty liver disease, a common comorbidity even in T1D
  • GGT (gamma-glutamyl transferase): Improvements in this liver health marker, reflecting reduced oxidative stress in hepatocytes
  • For someone on GLP-1 already, transitioning to Reta could enhance these liver-protective effects substantially

Additional Metabolic Benefits

  • Blood pressure: Modest but consistent reductions in both systolic and diastolic BP, reducing cardiovascular strain
  • Uric acid: Improvements observed, reducing gout risk and reflecting better kidney metabolic processing
  • Waist circumference: Dramatic reductions (some studies showing 5+ inches) — the most practical measure of visceral fat loss
  • Appetite regulation: Superior satiety signaling compared to single-agonist GLP-1, making adherence to the 1,880 calorie target easier
  • Energy expenditure: The glucagon receptor activation increases resting metabolic rate — you burn more calories at rest, even in a caloric deficit

How to Bring This Up with Your Endocrinologist

  • Print this section or show it on your phone at your next endo appointment
  • Ask: "I'm currently on a GLP-1 agonist. Given my T1D and recomp goals, would transitioning to Retatrutide be appropriate?"
  • Mention your structured coaching program (training, nutrition, peptides) — your endo will appreciate that you have professional support
  • Key concern to address: insulin dosing adjustments during transition — Reta's improved insulin sensitivity may require reducing basal/bolus insulin to avoid hypoglycemia
  • If Reta is not yet available through your endo, ask about Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) as a dual-agonist intermediate step
  • Important: Do NOT stop your current GLP-1 or change doses without your endocrinologist's guidance. This is a discussion, not a self-prescribed change.

Daily Temperature Check

METABOLIC TRACKING

Why We Track Your Morning Temperature

Your waking body temperature is one of the most underused and powerful biomarkers in coaching. It gives us a direct, daily window into your metabolic rate, thyroid function, recovery status, and overall hormonal health — all without a blood draw. This single data point, tracked consistently, can reveal problems weeks before they show up on lab work or the scale.

How to Take Your Temperature

  • When: Immediately upon waking, before getting out of bed, before drinking water, before any activity
  • How: Use a digital oral thermometer. Place under your tongue for the full reading cycle (usually 30–60 seconds). Do not talk or breathe through your mouth.
  • Where to log: Record it in your daily check-in in the FBF app under the body/notes section
  • Keep your thermometer on your nightstand — if you forget and get up first, the reading is no longer valid for that day
  • Consistency is key: Same thermometer, same method, every single morning. The trend over days and weeks is what matters, not any single reading.

What Your Temperature Tells Us

Metabolic Rate & Thyroid Function

Your basal body temperature is a direct reflection of your metabolic rate — how much energy your body is producing at rest. Thyroid hormones (T3 and T4) are the primary regulators of this process. They control how every cell in your body converts nutrients into heat and energy.

  • Healthy range: 97.8°F – 98.6°F upon waking indicates a well-functioning metabolism and adequate thyroid output
  • Consistently below 97.6°F is a red flag for metabolic downregulation — your body is slowing its engine to conserve energy. This is the earliest warning sign that calories may be too low, the deficit has been running too long, or thyroid function is suppressed.
  • Why this matters for you (T1D): Type 1 diabetics are at higher risk for thyroid dysfunction (Hashimoto's, hypothyroidism) due to autoimmune overlap. Catching a temperature decline early lets us intervene before it becomes a clinical issue.
  • If we see a sustained drop, we can increase calories (refeed), adjust carb timing, or flag it for your endocrinologist to check thyroid labs — all before you hit a plateau or feel run down.

Adaptive Thermogenesis & Diet Response

When you diet — especially in a caloric deficit — your body can fight back through a process called adaptive thermogenesis. It literally turns down the thermostat to burn fewer calories and protect its fat stores. This is the #1 reason diets stall.

  • A gradual temperature decline (0.3–0.5°F over 2–3 weeks) while in a deficit tells us your metabolism is adapting. This is normal to a degree, but if it drops too far or too fast, we need to act.
  • What we do about it: Strategic refeed days (temporary calorie increase, especially carbs) to signal to your body that it's not starving. This resets leptin signaling and bumps thermogenesis back up.
  • Why the recomp approach helps: By keeping your deficit moderate (1,880 cal, not extreme) and protein very high (200g), we minimize adaptive thermogenesis. Your body has less reason to panic because it's getting enough fuel and building blocks.
  • GLP-1 interaction: GLP-1 medications can affect thermoregulation. Tracking temperature helps us distinguish between normal GLP-1 effects and genuine metabolic suppression that needs intervention.

Recovery, Stress & Overtraining

Your temperature also reflects your autonomic nervous system balance — the interplay between your sympathetic ("fight or flight") and parasympathetic ("rest and digest") systems.

  • A sudden temperature drop (0.5°F+ from your baseline) after a hard training week can indicate overreaching — your nervous system is fatigued and recovery systems are taxed
  • A spike above your normal range can indicate acute stress, inflammation, infection onset, or an immune response. For a T1D client, this also correlates with blood sugar instability.
  • Pattern recognition: Over 4–6 weeks of data, we'll know your personal baseline. Then deviations become incredibly informative — we can see when your body needs a deload week, more sleep, or a protocol adjustment before you even feel it subjectively.
  • Cortisol connection: Chronically elevated cortisol (from under-recovery, poor sleep, or life stress) suppresses thyroid conversion (T4→T3) and lowers waking temperature. If we see this pattern, we address stress and recovery, not just training volume.

Hormonal Health & Peptide Response

Temperature is a downstream marker of your entire hormonal cascade — not just thyroid, but testosterone, growth hormone, insulin sensitivity, and more.

  • Tracking response to peptides: When we introduce CJC/Ipamorelin and Tesamorelin, we expect to see improved sleep, better recovery, and a stable or slightly elevated waking temperature over 4–6 weeks. If temperature doesn't respond, it tells us the peptide dose or timing may need adjustment.
  • Testosterone correlation: Low waking temperature combined with fatigue, low motivation, and stalled strength gains can indicate low testosterone — something we'd flag for bloodwork to confirm.
  • Insulin sensitivity marker: Better insulin sensitivity (from training, nutrition, GLP-1, and potential Reta) typically corresponds with a more stable, higher waking temperature. As your metabolic health improves, your temperature should gradually settle into the upper end of the healthy range.
  • Sleep quality feedback loop: Peptides like CJC/Ipamorelin enhance deep sleep, which is when your body does the most recovery and hormone production. Improved sleep → better hormone output → higher waking temperature. If temperature isn't trending up after starting peptides, we investigate sleep quality first.
