Compound Recommendations

PHYSICIAN GUIDED

Retatrutide (Reta) — The Metabolic Game-Changer

Retatrutide is a triple-hormone receptor agonist (GIP/GLP-1/Glucagon) that represents the most powerful metabolic compound available. At 300 lbs with 60 lbs to lose, Reta is the difference between a grind and a transformation. It crushes appetite, torches visceral fat, improves every metabolic marker, and makes adherence to this meal plan feel effortless.

Retatrutide — Why It Matters for Logan

  • Appetite Suppression: The single biggest reason recomps fail is hunger. Reta eliminates this problem — you'll have to remind yourself to eat, not fight cravings.
  • Visceral Fat Targeting: The glucagon receptor activation specifically targets deep abdominal fat — the metabolically dangerous stuff that drives inflammation, insulin resistance, and cardiovascular risk.
  • Metabolic Rate Preservation: Unlike pure caloric restriction, Reta's glucagon activation increases resting metabolic rate — you burn more at rest even in a deficit.
  • Clinical Results: Phase 2 trials showed up to 24% body weight reduction at 48 weeks. At your starting weight, that could mean 70+ lbs.
  • Cholesterol/Lipids: Significant reductions in LDL, triglycerides, and inflammatory markers across all dose groups.
  • Liver Health: Direct hepatic fat oxidation via glucagon receptor — dramatically reduces liver fat content.

Reta Titration Protocol (Discuss with Your Doctor)

  • Week 1–4: 1mg/week subcutaneous injection
  • Week 5–8: 2mg/week
  • Week 9–12: 4mg/week
  • Week 13+: 8mg/week (maintenance) or as tolerated
  • Inject once weekly — same day each week. Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thigh).
  • GI side effects (nausea, reduced appetite) are most common during titration. They subside. Stay hydrated.

Tesofensine — The Thermogenic Accelerator

  • Mechanism: Triple monoamine reuptake inhibitor — blocks reuptake of serotonin, dopamine, and noradrenaline. This increases resting metabolic rate, suppresses appetite through a completely different pathway than Reta, and enhances motivation/mood.
  • Why It Pairs with Reta: Reta works through gut hormones (GLP-1/GIP/Glucagon). Tesofensine works through brain neurotransmitters. Two completely different mechanisms = synergistic fat loss without diminishing returns.
  • Thermogenic Effect: Clinical trials showed ~160 kcal/day increase in resting metabolic rate — that's an extra pound of fat loss every 3 weeks just from existing.
  • Mood & Energy: The dopamine reuptake inhibition helps with the mental side of a big cut — better focus, motivation, and drive. Huge for an engineer who needs cognitive performance.
  • Dosing: 0.25–0.5mg/day oral. Start at 0.25mg. Take in the morning — it can affect sleep if taken late.
  • Monitoring: Can increase heart rate and blood pressure modestly. Track both weekly. Discuss with your physician.

TRT (Testosterone Replacement) — Discuss with Your Doctor

  • Why to Consider: At 300 lbs in your mid-30s, there's a high probability your testosterone is suboptimal. Excess body fat converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase. Sedentary desk work, stress, and poor sleep compound this. Low T = harder fat loss, less muscle building, worse mood, lower energy.
  • Step 1 — Get Labs: Request a comprehensive hormone panel: Total Testosterone, Free Testosterone, SHBG, Estradiol (sensitive), LH, FSH, Prolactin, TSH, Free T3/T4, CBC, CMP, Lipid Panel, HbA1c, Fasting Insulin.
  • What to Look For: Total T below 400 ng/dL and/or Free T in the bottom quartile of the range — especially with symptoms (fatigue, brain fog, low libido, difficulty losing fat) — makes a strong case for TRT.
  • If Prescribed: Typical protocol is 100–200mg testosterone cypionate per week, split into 2 injections (SubQ). Your doctor will dial in dosing based on labs at 6-week follow-up.
  • Impact on Recomp: Optimized testosterone is a force multiplier — it dramatically accelerates muscle protein synthesis, improves fat oxidation, enhances recovery, and elevates mood/motivation. Combined with your training program and peptide stack, this completes the hormonal foundation.
  • Important: Get bloodwork FIRST. Do not start TRT without a physician's supervision and baseline labs. We want data, not guesses.

Recommended Lab Panel — Get This Done Week 1

  • Hormones: Total Testosterone, Free Testosterone, SHBG, Estradiol (sensitive), LH, FSH, Prolactin, DHEA-S, IGF-1
  • Thyroid: TSH, Free T3, Free T4
  • Metabolic: Fasting Glucose, Fasting Insulin, HbA1c, CMP (kidney/liver function)

  • Vitamins: Vitamin D (25-OH), B12, Ferritin
  • Request this from your PCP or use a direct-to-consumer lab (Marek Health, Quest, LabCorp). Get a baseline NOW so we can track every marker as the program progresses.

Coach Recommendations

Lifestyle Optimization — The 20% That Drives 80% of Results

  • Sleep 7–8 Hours: This is where muscle is built and fat is burned. With a young family, this is hard — but it's the single most impactful variable. No screens 30 min before bed. Magnesium + casein pudding = your nighttime ritual. Dark, cool room.
  • Standing Desk or Desk Converter: You're an engineer — you're sitting 8–10 hours. Get a standing desk converter (~$200–300) and alternate 30 min sitting / 30 min standing. This alone can burn 200+ extra calories/day via NEAT.
  • Walking Meetings: Take every call you can while walking. AirPods + walk = steps + productivity. This is how you hit 10K steps without carving out extra time.
  • Train Early or at Lunch: With a young family, evening workouts get blown up by dinner, bath time, bedtime routines. Lock in a morning or lunch slot that's non-negotiable. 60 minutes, in and out.
  • Weigh Daily, Track Weekly: Step on the scale every morning (fasted, after bathroom). Don't react to daily fluctuations. Track the weekly average. That's your real number.
  • Progress Photos: Same angle, same lighting, same time (morning, fasted) — every 2 weeks. At 300 lbs with muscle memory, you will see changes faster than you think. These photos will be your motivation when the scale stalls.
  • InBody Scan: Get a baseline InBody scan (free at most gyms) in Week 1. Repeat monthly. This tracks lean mass vs fat mass — the scale doesn't tell the whole story during a recomp.
  • Stress Management: Engineering + young family = high cortisol. Chronically elevated cortisol blocks fat loss and promotes visceral fat storage. The training, sleep, and peptide stack all help — but also consider 5–10 min of breathwork or a short walk when stress peaks.

What to Expect — Setting Honest Expectations

  • Week 1–2: Water weight drops fast (5–8 lbs). You'll feel hungry before Reta fully kicks in. Muscles will be sore — you haven't done this in a while. This is normal. Push through.
  • Week 3–4: Reta titrating up, appetite fading. Energy stabilizing. Strength increasing rapidly (muscle memory). You'll start to feel like yourself again.
  • Week 5–8: This is where it gets fun. Clothes fitting different. Face thinning. People start noticing. Lifts jumping every session. The athlete is waking back up.
  • Week 9–12: Deep transformation territory. 20–30 lbs down. Significant lean mass regained. You look and feel like a different person. Labs should show dramatic improvements.
  • Week 13–24: We'll adjust macros, training volume, and compound dosing based on progress. Target: 240 lbs at a body composition that makes the old 300 unrecognizable.
  • The Scale Lie: There will be weeks where the scale doesn't move but your waist shrinks and your lifts go up. That's recomp — you're losing fat and building muscle simultaneously. Trust the process. Trust the photos. Trust the measurements.
