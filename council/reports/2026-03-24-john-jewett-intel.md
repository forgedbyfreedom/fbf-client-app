## TRAINING PHILOSOPHY

### Programming Approach
- **Split**: Push / Pull / Legs, isolation day added during prep
- **Rep Range**: 8-12 primary, 6-10 compounds, 12-20 isolation
- **RPE**: Programs at ~7.5 RPE, all work sets to muscular failure
- **Frequency**: Each muscle 2x/week in PPL
- **Volume**: Individualized — learned he needs LESS chest volume (better recruitment over time), MORE delt volume
- **Progressive Overload**: Tracks 4-6 week trends, not session-to-session. Considers execution quality improvement a form of overload

### Intensity Techniques
- **Rest-Pause**: Rep to failure → 20 sec rest → failure → 20 sec → failure
- **Reverse Pyramid**: Heavy set first (8-10), lighter high-rep set (15-18)
- **Slow Eccentrics**: Especially on pressing movements
- **AMRAP Finishers**: On isolation work

### The X-Frame Physique Concept
His signature: **wide lats + capped delts + sweeping quads + tight waist**
- 16-week hypertrophy program
- Wide-grip pulldowns for back width
- High lateral raise volume for capped delts
- Narrow-stance leg work for quad sweep
- Core/vacuum work for tight midsection

### Exercise Selection by Body Part

**Chest:**
- Hammer Strength Flat & Decline Press
- Incline Smith Machine Press
- Incline Cable Flyes
- Wider grip = more pec recruitment
- Keeps volume moderate (better fiber recruitment over time)

**Shoulders:**
- Smith Machine Military Press (slow eccentric)
- Cable Upright Row (rest-pause)
- Cable Lateral Raise (behind-the-back variation)
- **Cue: "Drive pinky up first"** for internal rotation = more side delt
- Laterals FIRST in session when energy highest

**Back (3 primary exercises):**
1. Lat-biased pulldowns — **"Drive elbow down and in toward hip, core braced like getting punched"**
2. Lat-biased rows — elbows tight to body
3. Upper-back rows — rear delts, traps, teres major

**Legs:**
- Quads: Front squats, hack squats, leg extensions (15-20 reps). KISS principle.
- Glutes/Hams: Back squat, RDLs, lying leg curls, glute bridges
- Hip abductor machine work

**Arms:**
- Cable overhead tricep extensions supersetted with dips
- Preacher curls, barbell curls, hammer curls

**Abs:**
- Concentric AND eccentric focus
- Vacuum practice
- Hanging leg raises, wood chops
- Hit failure, rest <1 min between sets

## NUTRITION METHODOLOGY

### Macro Framework
- **Men**: Start at 14 kcal/lb
- **Women**: Start at 12 kcal/lb
- **Protein**: 1.1-1.4 g/lb of fat-free mass (higher end during prep)
- **Fat**: 25% of total calories, never below 15%
- **Carbs**: Remaining calories
- Adjustments in 10% increments, monitor 1 week before re-adjusting

### Offseason
- 6 meals/day: ~50g protein, 80-90g carbs, 15-20g fats each
- One untracked free meal/week after 4-8 week reverse
- Staples: sirloin, chicken, eggs, jasmine rice, oats, sweet potatoes, peanut butter, sauerkraut

### Carb Cycling
- Alternates high/moderate/low days based on training demands
- Warns rest-day carbs too low impairs recovery
- Moderate adjustments, not extreme

### Reverse Dieting — AGGRESSIVE Approach
- Cuts cardio in half immediately post-show
- Raises food concurrently
- Rationale: Slow reverse keeps you in deficit, wastes growth time
- Goal: Get into productive building state ASAP

### Peak Week — Anti-Extreme
- No rapid carb loads, water tapers, or sodium manipulation
- **"Eat into the show"** — raise carbs, taper cardio, drop fatigue
- Stay hydrated throughout
- Walk onstage full, hard, hydrated

1. **"Whatever built the muscle retains the muscle"** — don't overhaul training during prep
2. **Progressive overload = execution quality**, not just more weight
3. **Track 4-6 week trends**, not session-to-session
4. **Exercise order reflects priorities** — lagging parts first
5. **Machines outperform free weights** for hypertrophy in many contexts
6. **Form breakdown = failure** of the intended working muscle
7. **Eccentric control is the game changer**
8. **Volume is individual** — adjusts per muscle based on recruitment ability
9. **Deloading isn't quitting — it's how you grow**
10. **"Effort is the main predictor in reaching your full potential"**
11. **KISS principle for legs**
12. **Monitor offseason red flags**: sleep, digestion, resting HR, BP, labs
